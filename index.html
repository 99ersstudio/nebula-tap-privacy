1. Who We Are & Contact

Nebula Tap: Space Idle Clicker is published by 99ers Studio (the data controller). You can reach us at any time at 99ers.dev@gmail.com for privacy questions, data access requests, or account deletion requests.

2. Data We Collect

a) Game Progress (on-device only)

Your upgrade levels, credit totals, mission progress, settings, audio and haptic preferences, and animation-quality selection are stored in private app storage on your device using Android SharedPreferences. This data is not transmitted to our servers.

b) Anonymous Firebase User ID (UID)

When the online leaderboard is enabled, Firebase Authentication creates a random anonymous identifier (UID) so your score entry can be stored and updated. The UID is not linked to an email, phone number, name, or any real-world identity. No login credentials are collected.

c) Leaderboard Record

If you participate in the online leaderboard, the following are written to Google Cloud Firestore and are visible to other players: the display name you choose in-app (optional), your total credits earned, your current tier/division, and a timestamp. You may clear this record at any time (see Section 10).

d) Crash & Stability Diagnostics

Firebase Crashlytics collects crash stack traces, device model, operating-system version, app version, locale, and limited runtime state when the app crashes or encounters a non-fatal error. We use Crashlytics to identify and fix bugs. Reports do not include the contents of your saved game, your UID, or any information you have typed into the app.

e) Advertising Data (Google AdMob)

The Google Mobile Ads SDK (AdMob) is used to show banner and rewarded ads. In line with Google's published data disclosure for AdMob, the SDK automatically collects and processes the following categories of data for ad serving, ad measurement, and fraud prevention:

  • Advertising ID (Android AD_ID)
  • IP address
  • Approximate (non-precise) location derived from IP
  • User interactions with ads (impressions, clicks, viewability)
  • Diagnostic information and device/network identifiers (device model, OS version, app version, connectivity type)

AdMob does not serve personalized ads to users who have not consented where consent is required (EEA/UK/Switzerland, US state privacy laws). Consent is collected in-app via the Google User Messaging Platform (UMP) consent dialog.

f) What We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or physical address.
  • We do not collect precise (GPS) location.
  • We do not access contacts, photos, microphone, camera, or files.
  • We do not collect health, financial, or biometric data.
  • We do not collect browsing history or search history.
  • The app has no in-app purchases and does not process payment information.

5. Data Retention

  • Local game progress: retained on your device until you reset it or uninstall the app.
  • Leaderboard record (UID, display name, score): retained in Firestore until you request deletion or until the record is pruned by periodic maintenance.
  • Crash reports: retained for up to 90 days (Firebase Crashlytics default), then deleted.
  • AdMob data (AD_ID, IP, ad interactions): retained by Google per the Google Ads data-retention policy.

6. International Data Transfers

The third-party services listed above are operated by Google LLC and may process data on servers located in the United States and other countries. Where personal data originating in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland is transferred outside those regions, it is protected by the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses and/or other safeguards published by the service provider.

7. Data Security

All communication between the app and cloud services uses TLS (Transport Layer Security) encryption. Anonymous UIDs do not grant access to personal account data. Firestore security rules prevent one user from modifying another user's leaderboard record. No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Children & Target Audience

Nebula Tap is rated for a general audience and is not directed at children under 13 (or the minimum digital-consent age in your jurisdiction, such as 16 in some EEA countries). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, contact 99ers.dev@gmail.com and we will promptly delete it.

The app does not participate in Google Play's "Designed for Families" program. Personalized advertising is disabled and only non-personalized ads are shown when age-neutral ad-serving settings apply.

9. Your Rights & Choices

Depending on where you live, you have some or all of the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Deletion / Erasure — request deletion of your data (see Section 10).
  • Restriction & Objection — limit or object to certain processing (for example, crash reporting or ads).
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Withdraw consent — change your ad/consent choice at any time via the in-app consent dialog (Settings → Privacy choices).
  • Opt out of "sale" or "sharing" (CCPA/CPRA) — we do not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined under California law.
  • Lodge a complaint — EEA/UK residents may contact their local supervisory authority.

In-app controls:

  • Reset game progress via Settings → Reset Progress.
  • Reconfigure consent via Settings → Privacy choices (re-opens the UMP consent dialog).
  • Manage or reset your device Advertising ID via Android's Settings → Google → Ads.

10. Account & Data Deletion

The only "account" Nebula Tap creates is an anonymous Firebase UID used for the leaderboard. You can remove all associated data in either of the following ways:

In-app: open Settings → Reset Progress. This clears your on-device save and removes your leaderboard record on the next sync.

By email: send a request to 99ers.dev@gmail.com with the subject "Nebula Tap — Data Deletion Request". We will confirm receipt and complete deletion within 30 days. We do not require you to create an account or provide additional personal information to make this request.

What gets deleted: your Firestore leaderboard document (UID, display name, score, tier), any Crashlytics reports tied to your install (where identifiable), and all on-device save data.
What does not get deleted: aggregate, de-identified analytics that no longer identify you; data we are required by law to retain.

If we make material changes to this policy — for example, by adding a new SDK or a new category of data — we will update the effective date above, note the change in the Google Play store listing's "What's new" section for the next release, and, where required, present an in-app notice.
